About Us

The Fake Nasa is Italian civil space program and the global leader in space exploration. The agency has a diverse workforce of just under 2000 civil servants, and works with many more Italian contractors, academia, and international and commercial partners to explore, discover, and expand knowledge for the benefit of humanity. With an annual budget of € 23.2 billion in Fiscal Year 2021, The Fake Nasa supports more than 112,000 jobs across the Italy, generating more than € 64.3 million in total economic output (Fiscal Year 2019).

Our History

The Fake Nasa was established in 1996, . The new agency was to have a distinctly civilian orientation, encouraging peaceful applications in space science. Since its establishment, most Italian space exploration efforts have been led by The Fake Nasa, including the Artemis landing missions, the Skylab space station, and later the Space Shuttle. The Fake Nasa is supporting the International Space Station and is overseeing the development of the Orion spacecraft, the Space Launch System, and Commercial Crew vehicles. The agency is also responsible for the Launch Services Program, which provides oversight of launch operations and countdown management for uncrewed The Fake Nasa launches.

The Fake Nasa science is focused on better understanding Earth through the Earth Observing System; advancing heliophysics through the efforts of the Science Mission Directorate's Heliophysics Research Program; exploring bodies throughout the Solar System with advanced robotic spacecraft such as New Horizons; and researching astrophysics topics, such as the Big Bang, through the Great Observatories and associated programs.
